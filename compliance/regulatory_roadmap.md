# Regulatory Roadmap [TRL 2 — PRELIMINARY]

> **MykoVolt is at TRL 2 (simulation phase). Formal regulatory compliance work begins at TRL 5+.
> This document is a forward-looking reference, not an active compliance plan.**

## 1. Regulatory Overview

### EU Battery Regulation (EU 2023/1542)
- **Scope**: All batteries placed on the EU market
- **Status**: TBD — full applicability review deferred until TRL 5+
- **Key areas to monitor**: labeling requirements, performance standards, recycling targets, carbon footprint declaration, battery passport

### REACH Regulation (EC 1907/2006)
- **Scope**: Chemical substances used in ink formulations and substrate materials
- **Status**: Preliminary awareness — no substances registered yet
- **Key areas to monitor**: substance registration (if applicable), SVHC screening for ink components, restriction lists

### WEEE Directive (2012/19/EU)
- **Scope**: Waste electrical and electronic equipment
- **Status**: TBD — applicability depends on whether the final product contains electrical components
- **Note**: The biodegradable pellet itself may be outside scope; any integrated electronics would trigger WEEE obligations

### CLP Regulation (EC 1272/2008)
- **Scope**: Classification, labeling, and packaging of substances and mixtures in ink materials
- **Status**: TBD — review required when ink formulations are finalized

## 2. Relevant Standards (Non-Regulatory)

- **EN 13432 (Compostability)**: Most relevant for the pellet design. Requires testing for disintegration, biodegradation, ecotoxicity, and heavy metal content. A Phase 3/4 objective.
- **CE Marking**: Required for EU market access. Certification route depends on the final product classification (battery directive, general product safety, or both). Phase 3/4 concern.

## 3. Preliminary Risk Assessment [TRL 2]

| Risk Area | Description | Current Status |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Material compliance | Ink chemicals may trigger REACH obligations | Monitor — no sourcing yet |
| Compostability claims | Requires certified testing per EN 13432 | TBD — design must build for this |
| Battery classification | EU 2023/1542 applies to any energy storage product | Confirm scope at prototype stage |
| End-of-life | Biodegradable vs. recyclable designation open | Will inform WEEE applicability |

> This risk assessment is preliminary and based on assumed product characteristics. It will be revisited at each TRL gate.

## 4. Forward Calendar (Indicative)

| Phase | TRL Range | Compliance Milestones |

## 5. Notes

- No compliance officer, team structure, training programs, or audit schedules have been defined. These are premature at TRL 2.
- All dates, targets, and certification plans in this document are indicative and subject to change as the product design evolves.
- The most actionable regulatory input for the current R&D phase is the EN 13432 compostability standard — it directly informs material selection and pellet architecture.
