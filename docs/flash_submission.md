# FLASH Submission: MykoVolt — Fungal Bio-Battery for Disposable IoT Sensors

## The Idea (298 words)

**Problem:** Millions of disposable IoT sensors (soil monitoring, cold-chain logistics, structural health) use Li-ion batteries that create toxic e-waste and require costly retrieval. Solar fails underground or in darkness.

**Solution:** MykoVolt develops the world's first biodegradable fungal bio-battery — a microbial fuel cell (MFC) that powers sensors using living fungi.

**How it works:** Two fungal species work together:
- *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* (yeast) at the anode releases electrons during metabolism
- *Trametes pubescens* (white-rot fungus) at the cathode produces laccase enzyme, accepting electrons and reducing oxygen to water
- The fungi are embedded in a cellulose-based conductive matrix, compression-molded into a pellet
- The system is dry-storable and activated on-site with water + nutrients

**Key innovation:** While fungal MFCs exist in labs (Empa baseline: 12.5 µW/cm²), no one has combined high-performance fungal strains with scalable manufacturing (compression molding, not 3D printing) for fully biodegradable, drop-and-forget sensor networks.

**Applications:** Underground soil sensors, compost monitoring, concrete curing sensors, cold-chain loggers — anywhere batteries can't be retrieved or light is unavailable.

**Impact:**
- 90% biodegradable (only electronics retrieved, reused 100+ cycles)
- €0.15/unit cost vs. €2-5 for Li-ion
- Zero e-waste, zero retrieval labor
- Enables dense sensor networks (100+ sensors/hectare) previously impossible

**Status:** Design complete. Recruiting team (CTO, Master's students) to build first functional prototype Q4 2026. Planning grant applications (Hessen Ideen, EXIST, Horizon Europe).

**Why now?** EU Green Deal pushes circular economy; IoT sensor market exploding; e-waste crisis demands alternatives. MykoVolt enables sensors deployed "like seeds" — compostable, underground, zero maintenance.
